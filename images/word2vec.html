A three-dimensional embedding of the main sample of galaxy spectra from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (approximately 675,000 spectra observed in 3750 dimensions). Colors in the above figure indicate the strength of Hydrogen alpha emission, a very nonlinear feature which requires dozens of dimensions to be captured in a linear embedding.

3,000,000 words and phrases mapped by word2vec using Google News into 300 dimensions. The data was then embedded into 2 dimensions using Spectral Embedding. The plot shows a sample of 10,000 points displaying the overall shape of the embedding as well as the estimated “stretch” (i.e. dual push-forward Riemannian metric) at various locations in the embedding.
